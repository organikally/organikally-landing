## The market truth this page must answer

Indian mustard-oil buyers carry a specific, well-earned anxiety: **adulteration** (the
historic argemone-oil scare) and "organic" used as an empty label. The premium buyer isn't
paying for a logo — they're paying to *stop worrying*. The page wins by making purity and
process **visible and verifiable**, not merely asserted. Secondary truth: mustard oil is
emotional — *kachi ghani* pungency, the smell of a home kitchen, regional memory.

## Primary persona — Meera, the health-led home cook (38, Tier-1 metro)

- **Jobs:** feed her family food she trusts; cook authentic Indian meals with the right
  pungency; lower the background anxiety about adulterated/refined oils.
- **Triggers:** a health scare in the family, a label-reading habit, a friend's recommendation.
- **Objections → proof she needs:**
  - "Is this *actually* organic or just marketing?" → named sourcing, certification/FSSAI,
    process transparency.
  - "Could it be adulterated?" → cold-press (kachi ghani) explanation, purity statement,
    test/lab or cert signal, glass/quality packaging cues.
  - "Is cold-pressed worth the higher price?" → MUFA/Omega-3 + antioxidants framed plainly;
    nutrient-retention story of cold extraction vs refining.
  - "Will it smell/taste right?" → sensory language (aroma, pungency), usage for Indian cooking.
- **Decision style:** reads, compares, then commits and repurchases. High LTV.

## Secondary persona — Arjun/Priya, the diaspora nostalgist & gifter (32)

- **Jobs:** recreate the taste of home; gift something wholesome and premium to parents/elders;
  back an authentic Indian brand they're proud of.
- **Objections → proof:** "Will it taste like back home?" → regional authenticity + heritage
  story. "Is it premium enough to gift / give my parents?" → craft of the brand, presentation,
  trust signals. "Can I rely on it?" → range breadth (oil + dals + khand) signals a real brand.
- **Note:** abroad, mustard oil is often sold "for external use only" — diaspora know the
  difference and value a brand that speaks to authentic culinary use.

## Tertiary persona — the conscious trade buyer (kirana / organic store / HoReCa / corporate gifting)

- **Jobs:** stock a trustworthy organic range with documentation; reliable supply; healthy
  margin; certs they can show their own customers.
- **Objections → proof:** "Licences & certs?" → FSSAI + organic certification visible.
  "Breadth & consistency?" → the growing range + an enquiry/wholesale path. "Support?" →
  a clear B2B contact route (WhatsApp/enquire).

## Implications for the build

- Trust must be **shown early and often** — process + provenance + certification beat slogans.
- Lead with the hero product (mustard oil) but signal the **range** so the brand reads as real.
- Sensory + heritage language carries the diaspora; verifiable purity carries the home cook.
- Provide a **low-friction enquiry path** (WhatsApp-first likely) that serves both D2C and B2B.
